About Liberation War 1971

A respectful overview of why the war started, and who stood for freedom.

Why the War Started

In 1971, Bangladesh (then East Pakistan) faced political oppression and widespread injustice. The people’s demand for self-determination grew into a nationwide resistance, leading to the Liberation War.

This struggle united ordinary civilians and freedom fighters with one goal: independence.

Key Leaders / Freedom Fighters

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman

A central figure in the independence movement and a symbol of leadership.

General M. A. G. Osmani

Leadership of the Bangladesh forces during the war.

Freedom Fighters (Collective)

Thousands of fighters, organizers, and civilians contributed to the struggle.

Women of the Liberation War

Women courageously served as fighters, supporters, and protectors.
